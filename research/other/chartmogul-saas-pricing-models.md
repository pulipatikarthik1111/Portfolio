# ChartMogul — A guide to SaaS pricing models (summary)

Source: `https://chartmogul.com/blog/saas-pricing-models/`  
Collected: 2026-04-15

## Executive summary

Pricing performance often fails due to **packaging/model mismatch**, not “price too high.” The article reviews eight common SaaS pricing models and emphasizes aligning pricing with **value drivers** (usage, seats, features) while keeping the structure understandable. The overall trend is toward **hybrid** and **more granular** pricing as products mature and customers demand flexibility.

## 8 common SaaS pricing models (with quick notes)

1) **Tiered**
- Multiple plans (Basic/Pro/Enterprise) with different features/price points.
- Pros: flexible segmentation, upgrade paths, predictable tiers.
- Cons: packaging complexity; “in-between” customers; upgrade resentment.

2) **Flat-rate**
- One price for one feature set.
- Pros: simple; no usage tracking.
- Cons: limited scaling; misses upside from large customers; less negotiation room.

3) **Usage-based (pay-as-you-go)**
- Price scales with usage (API calls, volume, transactions).
- Pros: scales with value; affordable entry; aligns impact with value.
- Cons: revenue variability; hard usage estimation; surprise bills can churn.

4) **Credit-based**
- Buy credits upfront; spend credits on actions/features.
- Pros: upfront/bulk revenue; flexible for customers.
- Cons: confusing; hard to price credits; negotiation caps; tricky revenue accounting.

5) **Per-user / seat-based (incl. per-active-user)**
- Pay per seat.
- Pros: easy budgeting; grows with customer headcount; land-and-expand friendly.
- Cons: discourages broad adoption; login sharing; churn if adoption drops.

6) **Feature-based (add-ons)**
- Base plan + add-ons.
- Pros: customization; upsell; clear feedback on willingness-to-pay.
- Cons: can overwhelm buyers; requires product understanding; “all-in-one” competitors.

7) **Freemium**
- Free plan + paid upgrades.
- Pros: low friction; word-of-mouth loops; loyalty.
- Cons: resource drain; needs strong onboarding/upsell; abuse risk; positioning risk.

8) **Hybrid**
- Combines multiple models (often tiered + seats/credits/add-ons).
- Pros: flexible; scalable; experimentation-friendly.
- Cons: operational complexity; can feel confusing/hidden-cost.

## Strategic guidance (high-signal)

- **Embrace granular pricing**: customers have more power; rigid plan-only models often lose.
- **Quantify impact**: price should mirror value drivers (usage, permissions, outcomes).
- **If conversion is low**: don’t reflexively cut price—often buyers don’t understand value or the structure is too complex.
- **Hybrid is a default for mature products**: mix models to serve segments (SMB vs Enterprise) without racing to the bottom.

## Outreach / GTM note (how to use this)

When prospecting into pricing/RevOps leaders, map your message to the buyer’s current pricing pain:
- pricing confusion → “simplify packaging”
- expansion friction → “align price to value drivers”
- volatility risk → “predictable guardrails for usage-based”
