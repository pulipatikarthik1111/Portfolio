# ChartMogul — Revenue Operations 101 for SaaS Companies (summary)

Source: `https://chartmogul.com/blog/saas-revenue-operations-101/`  
Collected: 2026-04-15

## Executive summary

RevOps is not “magic”—it’s the operating system that aligns **Marketing + Sales + Customer Success** around shared revenue outcomes by fixing process, data, and tooling. Companies typically formalize RevOps when scale exposes pain: repetitive work, poor handoffs, siloed systems, inconsistent customer experience, and slowing growth.

## What is RevOps (definition)

Revenue Operations is a function that maximizes revenue by aligning teams that impact the bottom line, acting as the connective link across marketing, sales, and customer success.

## When to consider dedicated RevOps (signals + benchmarks)

Signals highlighted:
- Growth depends on headcount (linear scaling trap)
- Teams stuck doing repetitive tasks (time-cost compounding)
- Inconsistent customer experience (bad handoffs; marketing/sales/cs out of sync)
- Data siloed across teams/tools (no single source of truth)
- Growth slowing / “hitting a wall”

Benchmarks mentioned:
- Start thinking about RevOps once there’s a sales leader + 1–2 reps.
- Dedicated roles commonly appear around **100–150 employees**.

## Core responsibilities (what RevOps typically does)

- **CRM administration**
  - workflows, standard processes, training, dashboards
  - automation of manual data entry; increasing AI/automation in CRM workflows
- **Manage tech bloat**
  - audit stack, find duplication, utilization, renewals, procurement, integrations/migrations, adoption
- **Process optimization**
  - document SOPs; fix inefficiencies; establish cross-functional comms
  - especially marketing → sales handoff (qualification standards, required context)
- **Training & development**
  - onboarding teams to tools/processes; adoption of new systems
- **Revenue monitoring & forecasting**
  - centralize data; dashboards; bottom-line KPIs; forecasting; alignment across functions
- **“Fix what’s broken”**
  - ongoing operational firefighting in fast-changing GTM systems

## Metrics called out for RevOps dashboards

- **MRR movements**: New, Expansion, Reactivation, Contraction, Churned MRR
- Lead conversion rate (marketing → sales handoff health)
- Sales cycle time
- CAC
- Customer churn
- LTV
- Forecast accuracy

## Practical playbook takeaways (for our synthesis layer)

- RevOps value is highest where **handoffs** and **data hygiene** break down.
- “Single source of truth” enables consistent customer experience (stop selling to existing customers, respect comms prefs).
- Automation targets repetitive work first; set standards before tooling.
